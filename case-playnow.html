CASE FILE 001 / PLATFORM MODERNIZATION

Modernizing a billion-dollar engine without breaking the regulator's trust.

PlayNow.com was three businesses sharing one header — lottery, casino, and sports betting, all built on different mental models. We rebuilt it as a single, unified platform that could ship faster, scale further, and keep British Columbia's gaming regulator happy at every step.

01 / The Inheritance

When I joined, PlayNow was technically one website but operated like three. The lottery vertical was a 1990s brochure dressed up. The casino vertical was an embedded iframe with its own UI. Sports betting had been bolted on and lived in yet another visual language. A user who wanted to buy a Lotto 6/49 ticket and place a hockey wager had to learn three different interfaces and trust each one separately.

The technical reality was worse. Every product team had its own component library, its own auth tokens, and its own approach to compliance. Adding a new game took six months. Hot fixes took two weeks.

02 / Treating Design as an Operating System

The redesign that mattered wasn't visual — it was structural. We built a shared component library with compliance baked in: every interactive surface validated against responsible-gambling rules before it could ship. Auth, wallet, deposit, and limits were unified across all three verticals. One login. One wallet. One trusted environment.

That shift let squads launch HTML5 instant games and live casino three months faster than the previous cycle — not by skipping steps, but by inheriting them.

03 / High-Density UX, Low-Density Anxiety

Sports betting is a UX challenge in compressed form: thousands of live markets, odds that change by the second, and parlays that need to be built without breaking concentration. We replaced a click-heavy navigation with a quick-tap betslip that always stayed in context. Odds movement signals — colour-coded green and red — made volatility transparent rather than hidden.

Compliance guardrails (session timers, deposit limits, reality checks) were threaded into the wagering flow itself, not buried in a settings page. The regulator approved on the first review.

"The hardest part wasn't designing what we shipped. It was earning the right to ship it."

04 / Lottery, Reduced to its Essence

Historical analytics showed a 30% drop-off at the lottery selection step. Players wanted to play, but the interface kept asking them to make decisions they didn't have a mental model for. We redesigned around the two behaviours that mattered: Quick Pick (94% of plays) and Pick My Numbers (loyal heavy users). The manual flow now mirrors the physical play-slip — a metaphor people already trust.

05 / What Worked

  • Unified wallet & auth. One login moved users seamlessly between products. Deposits up 18%.
  • Compliance as design. Limits and session controls became part of the UX, not friction at the edges.
  • Componentized design system. Cut new-game launch time from 6 months to under 90 days.
  • Cross-sell discovery. A unified header surfaced sports during lottery flows — and vice versa.
  • Accessibility by default. WCAG 2.0 AA conformance baked into the base CSS, not retrofitted.

06 / What I Learned

Regulated environments don't reward cleverness — they reward predictability. The most powerful design move we made wasn't visual at all. It was treating every screen as a contract: between the player and the brand, between the brand and the regulator, between today's product and tomorrow's compliance audit. When the system is trusted at that level, growth follows.
