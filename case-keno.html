CASE FILE 002 / GAME REDESIGN

Keno — pacing, clarity, and the rhythm of a four-minute draw.

A legacy lottery game with deeply loyal players and a UX stuck in 2008. The challenge was to lift engagement without breaking the things existing players already trusted — and to do it inside a four-minute draw cycle that runs all day, every day.

01 / The Game's Real Surface

Keno's true UX isn't a screen — it's a tempo. A new draw fires every four minutes, all day, every day. The product has to make picking numbers, watching the draw, checking results, and deciding whether to play again all feel like one continuous loop. The original design treated each step as a separate page, breaking the rhythm and losing players at every transition.

02 / Research with Loyal Players

We sat with players in their homes — most of them in their 50s and 60s — and watched how they actually played. Almost all of them had a phone in one hand and a TV in the other. They wanted to half-watch the draw, with the option to lean in for the moments that mattered. Most didn't want a "modern" gambling app aesthetic. They wanted a tool that respected their pace.

"The players didn't want excitement engineered into the screen. They wanted the screen to get out of the way."

03 / Designing the Loop, Not the Page

We collapsed pick → confirm → watch → result into a single visual frame. The number grid, the live draw, and the result state share the same canvas — the interface transforms in place rather than cutting between screens. A clear countdown sits above every action. You always know exactly how much of the cycle is left.

For loyal players, we kept the option to favourite numbers and replay sets — the digital equivalent of a worn paper play-slip. For new players, we added a one-tap "Quick Pick + Auto-Play 5 draws" flow that dramatically lowered the cognitive cost of trying the game once.

04 / Transparent Pacing

A live progress meter shows the cycle. The brand voice in microcopy never tries to manufacture suspense; the suspense is the draw itself. Limits and time-spent indicators are visible but not nagging — part of the environment, not the interruption. When a player closes the game, the app tells them how long they played and how many draws they entered. No surprises, no shame.

05 / What Shipped

  • Single-frame loop. No page reloads between pick → draw → result.
  • Favorite sets. Saved number combinations replay in two taps.
  • Auto-Play. Run the same play across 5, 10, or 25 consecutive draws.
  • Visible cadence. Countdown ring, transparent draw status, calm result animation.
  • Responsible play, in-frame. Spend tracker visible without being shouted.

06 / The Result

Engagement doubled within three months of launch. Revenue lifted 68% year-over-year — and the support team's "I don't know what's happening" tickets dropped by nearly a quarter. The same players, on the same game, treated differently — and they responded.
